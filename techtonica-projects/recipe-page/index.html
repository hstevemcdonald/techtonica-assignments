Kale Citrus Quinoa salad

I created this salad when I was reading about "superfoods". I had been eating an almost 100% plant-based diet and wanted to ensure I was getting enough protein, as well as fiber and all the necessary vitamins and minerals. If you cook the quinoa yourself, be sure to rinse it very well, as it contains bitter-tasting substances called saponins. The dry grains have a tendency to fly around almost as if there is static pulling them onto whatever utensil you're using.

Ingredients

  • 4 cups of cooked quinoa
  • 1 tsp - 1 tbsp grated ginger
  • 1-3 cloves garlic (according to your preference)
  • 1/2 cup orange juice
  • 1/4 cup lime/lemon juice
  • 1-4 tbsp. extra-virgin olive oil (according to your preference)
  • 1-2 cups julienned kale
  • 1/4 cup diced red onion
  • 1/4 cup chopped parsley
  • 1/2 cup of garbanzo beans (aka chickpeas)
  • 1 cup pomegranate seeds or cherry tomato halves
  • optional: chopped walnuts
  • salt and pepper to taste

Directions for making the quinoa

  1. Wash the quinoa very very well
  2. Put into rice cooker with an equal amount of water
  3. Cook like rice

Directions for salad

  1. Put cooked quinoa into large bowl (enough to toss all ingredients without half of it falling out)
  2. Immediately add kale and mix well to let the kale wilt from the heat, and let cool
  3. Whisk garlic, juices and oil
  4. Drizzle over quinoa, mixing well so all the quinoa is coated
  5. Add next four ingredients (five if adding walnuts) and mix well
  6. Add salt and pepper to taste
